Teorema

  • Como \(V\) es definida positiva, existe su inversa \(V^{-1}\) y \(\mathbf a'V \mathbf a>0\) para todo vector \(\mathbf a\neq0\).
  • Entonces, tenemos \[\begin{align*} \frac{(\mathbf a^{\prime}\boldsymbol \mu_{\mathbf X}-\mathbf a^{\prime}\boldsymbol \mu_{\mathbf Y})^{2}}{\mathbf a^{\prime}V \mathbf a} & =\frac{(\mathbf a^{\prime}V^{1/2}V^{-1/2}(\boldsymbol \mu_{\mathbf X}-\boldsymbol \mu_{\mathbf Y}))^{2}}{\mathbf a^{\prime}V \mathbf a}\\ & \leq\frac{\mathbf a^{\prime}V \mathbf a(\boldsymbol \mu_{\mathbf X}-\boldsymbol \mu_{\mathbf Y})^{\prime}V^{-1}(\boldsymbol \mu_{\mathbf X}-\boldsymbol \mu_{\mathbf Y}% )}{\mathbf a^{\prime}V\mathbf a}\\ & =(\boldsymbol \mu_{\mathbf X}-\boldsymbol \mu_{\mathbf Y})^{\prime}V^{-1}(\boldsymbol \mu_{\mathbf X}-\boldsymbol \mu_{\mathbf Y})\\ &=d^2_V % (\boldsymbol \mu_{\mathbf X},\boldsymbol \mu_{\mathbf Y}), \end{align*}\] donde hemos considerado \(\mathbf x'=\mathbf a^{\prime}V^{1/2}\) e \(\mathbf y=V^{-1/2}(\boldsymbol \mu_{\mathbf X}-\boldsymbol \mu_{\mathbf Y})\).

Teorema

  • Entonces usando el teorema de la probabilidad total, se tiene \[\begin{align*} \Pr(error)&=\Pr(\mathbf Z\in R_{\mathbf Y}\mid \mathbf Z\equiv \mathbf X)\Pr(\mathbf Z\equiv \mathbf X) \\ & \quad \quad \quad +\Pr(\mathbf Z\in R_{\mathbf X}\mid \mathbf Z\equiv \mathbf Y)\Pr(\mathbf Z\equiv \mathbf Y) \\ &=\Pr(e_1)q_1+\Pr(e_2)q_2 \end{align*}\]

Criterio de mÃ¡xima probabilidad a posteriori
